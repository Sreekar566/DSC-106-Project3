What is a teleconnection? A teleconnection is a climate linkage between two regions that are far apart geographically — when temperatures in one region rise and fall, a connected region tends to follow the same pattern. An edge in this graph means those two regions' temperatures move together across the year. Thicker edges = stronger synchrony. This doesn't mean their climates are identical, just that they're statistically coupled — often driven by shared atmospheric circulation patterns or ocean currents.
